# Product

## Register

both

## Users

Students and indie developers, primarily in India, who want to experiment with frontier AI models (GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini) without committing to subscriptions, enterprise contracts, or foreign currency fees. They are budget-conscious but serious: building real side projects, learning by doing, or prototyping before scaling. They arrive frustrated by rate limits, billing delays, and annual plans designed for companies, not individuals.

## Product Purpose

AIRent is a pay-per-use AI API rental marketplace. Users rent a virtual API key for a time window (15 minutes to 24 hours), make API calls through an OpenAI-compatible endpoint, and pay only for what they use, in INR via UPI. The product removes every barrier between a developer and a frontier model: no approval, no KYC, no forex, no minimum spend. Success looks like a developer who has a working prototype running on a real model within 10 minutes of discovering AIRent.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, honest, indie. Feels like it was built by a developer who hit the same walls and decided to fix the problem rather than complain about it. Not corporate, not trying to look bigger than it is. Warm toward students and first-timers without being condescending. Confident in what it does without overselling.

Three words: grounded, capable, human.

## Anti-references

- **OpenAI / generic SaaS**: white backgrounds, blue accents, hero-metric grids, corporate polish that feels distant from the user.
- **Vercel / Linear dark mode**: pitch black, mono font, ultra-minimal. Oversaturated aesthetic lane; looks like every other dev tool.
- **Colorful startup**: rainbow gradients, bubbly shapes, Figma-style playfulness. Feels juvenile and mismatched to a developer audience.
- **Indian fintech apps**: heavy blues, badge-heavy layouts, information overload. The opposite of the calm confidence AIRent should project.

## Design Principles

1. **Practiced what it preaches.** The interface itself should feel like it was built with care and real craft, not scaffolded and shipped. If the product promises developer quality, the UI must demonstrate it.
2. **Friction is the enemy.** Every extra click, every unexplained field, every moment of confusion is a broken promise. The interface removes obstacles, it does not create them.
3. **Human scale.** This is a one-person product serving individuals. It should feel like talking to a knowledgeable friend, not filling out a corporate form. Copy is direct, honest, and never puffed up.
4. **Earned confidence.** No hero metrics, no "trusted by 10,000 developers" badges unless they are true. Let the product quality and transparency do the convincing.
5. **India-native, not India-lite.** INR, UPI, and Indian developer context are first-class, not afterthoughts. The product does not apologize for being made for and by India.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as a baseline. Keyboard navigation, visible focus states, sufficient contrast ratios. Reduced-motion support for users who have that preference set. No formal AAA requirement, but nothing that is gratuitously inaccessible.
